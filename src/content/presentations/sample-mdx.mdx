## Paragraphs and inline formatting

A regular paragraph can include **strong emphasis**, _italic emphasis_, **_combined emphasis_**, `inline code`, a [regular link](https://astro.build), and a [link with title text](https://docs.astro.build "Astro documentation").

Use inline semantic HTML when Markdown has no native equivalent: <mark>highlighted text</mark>, <kbd>Ctrl</kbd> + <kbd>K</kbd>, H<sub>2</sub>O, E = mc<sup>2</sup>, and <abbr title="HyperText Markup Language">HTML</abbr>.

Long text preview: Markdown content often includes sentences that wrap across multiple lines. This paragraph exists to show how the article width, line height, and contrast feel when readers scan a dense block of prose during a presentation review.

## Blockquotes

> A simple blockquote should stand apart from body text while still matching the article rhythm.

> **Tip:** Blockquotes can include formatting, links, and lists.
>
> - Keep the message focused.
> - Make important context visible.
> - Use nested quotes sparingly.
>
> > Nested blockquotes are useful for quoting a quote, but the visual indentation should remain readable.

## Escaped characters

Markdown can show punctuation that would otherwise become formatting: \*not emphasized\*, \_not italic\_, \`not code\`, and \[not a link\](https://example.com).

Use entities for angle brackets in prose: &lt;section&gt;, &lt;article&gt;, and &amp;.
